## Axis B — the kén-rể timeline question (THE headline finding)

### What canon says
The famous sính-lễ list **voi chín ngà, gà chín cựa, ngựa chín hồng mao** (+ cơm nếp / bánh chưng in the full version) and the rule **"ai đem lễ đến trước thì được vợ"** belong to **one specific contest**: Hùng Vương thứ 18 choosing a husband for **Mỵ Nương**, won by Sơn Tinh, after which Thủy Tinh — who arrived second — floods the plain **every year in revenge**. The flood is, by definition, the *aftermath* of a contest that has already concluded.

### What the corpus does
The corpus runs **two** kén-rể events on one timeline:

1. **The past, settled, divine contest** (Sơn Tinh vs Thủy Tinh, Mỵ Nương married, floods now recurring). Referenced as past throughout:
   - #01: *"Người ta bảo tại ngài Thủy Tinh năm xưa thua cuộc kén rể, ấm ức nên năm nào cũng lên đòi."* and *"Đã mười tám đời vua, năm nào hai ngài cũng so cao thấp một lần."* — unambiguously PAST + recurring. ✓
   - #03 op-ed: *"Bài học từ cuộc kén rể năm xưa cũng đã rõ: người mang lễ đến trước được vợ, kẻ đến sau ôm hận dâng nước mỗi năm."* — explicitly PAST. ✓
2. **A current, open contest** for *another* princess, using the *same* sính-lễ list and the *same* "ai đến trước" rule:
   - #18: *"Triều đình vừa thông báo chính thức mở đăng ký dự cuộc kén rể … voi chín ngà, gà chín cựa, ngựa chín hồng mao … ai đến trước sẽ được xét trước."*
   - #11: *"khi triều đình mở khoa thi kén rể cho công chúa, với yêu cầu lễ vật … voi chín ngà, gà chín cựa, ngựa chín hồng mao"* … *"trước ngày khoa thi."*

### Is this a contradiction? — Reasoned verdict
**It is NOT a hard contradiction, because the authors deliberately establish that #11/#18 is a NEW, SEPARATE contest, not a re-telling of the divine one.** The decisive evidence is in the comments, which are part of the published corpus:

- `khoa-thi-ken-re-mo-dang-ky.json` c5 (**Quan coi việc tuyển**): *"Đây là quy định cho **cuộc kén rể đặc biệt**, không phải lệ chung của dân."*
- same file c7 (**Bà mẹ làng Trung**): *"Mong **cuộc kén rể này có hậu hơn lần trước**."* — explicitly positions the current contest as a *later, distinct* event than the Sơn Tinh–Thủy Tinh one.
- #03 c4 (`goc-nhin…` Lạc tướng): *"cuộc kén rể năm xưa của vua là chuyện bất đắc dĩ … Cái sai là dân ta đem cái phép thử của thần ra làm tục lệ đại trà."* — the op-ed's own readership reads the two as separate.

So the **intended** in-world model is coherent: *past divine contest → eternal flood; a new royal kén-rể is now open for a different công chúa, and the people are (foolishly) copying the legendary sính-lễ.* That is actually a clever satirical premise (the bureaucracy fossilising a one-off divine ordeal into a recurring admissions ritual — the same frozen-dialect gag, applied to canon).

### Where it still breaks (the real, fixable defect — HIGH)
The problem is that the **article bodies of #11 and #18 never state that this is a new/different contest**, while reusing the *literally identical* sính-lễ triad and the *literally identical* "ai đến trước" tiebreak. To a Vietnamese reader who knows the tale, those four words ("voi chín ngà… ai đến trước") ARE the Sơn Tinh–Thủy Tinh contest. With no in-body disambiguation, #18 in particular reads as: *the contest that already happened (and whose loser floods us annually) is being opened for registration now.* The clarification currently lives only in a buried comment (#18 c5) and is **absent from the two pieces that most need it (#11, #18)**. #11 even calls it "khoa thi kén rể **cho công chúa**" — singular, generic — which a reader will map straight onto Mỵ Nương.

**Severity: HIGH** (it's the one thing most likely to read as "they got the legend's timeline wrong"). **Minimal fix** (do not implement — recommendation only):
- In **#18** body, add one clause naming it a *new* contest for a *different* princess, e.g. after the opening line: *"…mở đăng ký dự cuộc kén rể **cho một công chúa khác** của nhà vua — **không phải cuộc kén rể Mỵ Nương năm xưa** mà dân vẫn nhắc mỗi mùa lũ."* That single sentence converts the ambiguity into deliberate satire.
- In **#11** body, change "kén rể cho công chúa" → "kén rể cho **một** công chúa" and, ideally, one half-sentence echoing that this is the *new* contest, distinct from the divine one already referenced in #03.
- Optionally lampshade the reuse of the identical sính-lễ ("triều đình lại lấy đúng lễ vật huyền thoại năm xưa làm chuẩn") — this turns the overlap from a bug into the joke.

(If the editorial preference is the *opposite* reading — that #11/#18 ARE the original Mỵ Nương contest — then #01 and #03's "năm xưa / mỗi năm dâng nước" lines become the contradiction, and the flood lead cannot stand. The new-contest reading is far cheaper to make airtight, and is already what the comments assert.)

## Axis B — other chronology checks

### Post-Văn Lang sweep (An Dương Vương / Cổ Loa / nỏ thần / Triệu Đà / chữ Hán / tiền đúc)
- **No An Dương Vương, Cổ Loa, nỏ thần, Mỵ Châu–Trọng Thủy, or Triệu Đà** anywhere in articles or comments. The replacement of the nỏ-thần hồ sơ by #11 is clean. ✓
- **No minted coins** in articles. All exchange is barter (trâu, vò muối, đấu gạo, rìu đồng, thúng thóc). ✓
- **No chữ viết of our own.** The only "writing" is foreign (#10), see below. Mai An Tiêm "khắc dấu / đôi dòng cho biết xuất xứ" on melons (#07) is a *mark/sigil*, not literacy — canon-faithful (the legend has him carve marks). Acceptable. ✓
- **#10 "the-gioi-phuong-bac" — the one genuine grey zone.** It depicts a northern power "thâu tóm thiên hạ," standardising "một thứ cân, một thứ thước, **một lối viết**," ruling by harsh law — i.e. the **Qin (Tần) unification (221 BCE)**. CONTENT_NOTES names it "nhà Tần." Real-world, Qin's unification is *after* the traditional end of the Hùng line (Văn Lang → Âu Lạc ~258 BCE). **However:** (a) the article never names Tần, never names a writing system *for Văn Lang*, and frames everything as distant, multi-hop, unverifiable rumour ("tin còn rời rạc … khó kiểm chứng"); (b) it imports nothing post-Văn Lang *into* Văn Lang; (c) the "warring states gradually being swallowed" backdrop overlaps the *late* Hùng era anyway. **Verdict: acceptable as a deliberately-vague foreign-desk rumour, MEDIUM at most.** It is the only place a strict chronology hawk could object. If you want zero risk, soften "một lối viết" (the most era-specific tell) to a non-literacy marker like "một lối khắc dấu / một thứ luật" — keeps the Qin flavour without invoking script. (Severity: LOW–MEDIUM; reasoned-keep.)

### Mai An Tiêm contemporaneity (#07 vs #21) — MEDIUM
#07 is written as a *retrospective* ("đến nay vẫn được nhắc," "con cháu ông sau này") — i.e. Mai An Tiêm lived in the past. But #21 (and its comments, and #16) treat **"hàng vùng đảo Mai An Tiêm"** as a *live, currently-shipping* brand whose boats arrive "mấy phiên gần đây." A reader can reconcile this (the island/lineage keeps his trade and brand alive — like a heritage producer), and #21's bà Mây explicitly frames buying a melon as "nhớ ơn cái tích ông Mai An Tiêm" (homage, past tense). So it's defensible, but the two pieces lean opposite ways on whether the man himself is contemporary. **Minimal fix:** in #21, one word — "hàng vùng đảo **nối nghề** Mai An Tiêm" or "hàng vùng đảo **mang tích** Mai An Tiêm" — removes all doubt. (Severity: MEDIUM.)

### displayDate vs narrative ordering — one real slip (MEDIUM)
The flood lead **#01 is displayDated 12/7** and reports floodwaters *cresting then just beginning to chững* ("đà dâng đã có dấu hiệu chững lại từ giữa trưa"); its comments run 12–13/7 and end with water *starting to recede*. But **#11 (displayDate 14/7)** describes the chợ-phiên raid and a *bustling, dry, fully-operational* market with no floodwater, and its "dòng thời gian" places events on **mùng 5/7 and 13/7** — i.e. the sting operation and 4-gate lockdown happen *on 13/7, the morning after the flood peak*, with the market evidently back to normal. The flood (#01/#02) has Bạch Hạc-area markets "thưa," thuyền "mắc kẹt," and water only fully receding ~16/7 (#01 c16: "nước bắt đầu xuống được nửa bậc" on 13/7; #12/#13 say "nước vừa rút" around 16–18/7). A major chợ-phiên Kẻ Chủ raid in full swing on **13/7** sits awkwardly inside the flood window. It's not impossible (Kẻ Chủ may be on high ground away from the hợp-lưu), but nothing says so. **Severity: MEDIUM.** Minimal fix: either nudge #11's raid date to after the water has clearly receded (≥16/7), or add half a clause that chợ Kẻ Chủ sits on cao ráo and kept trading through the lũ.

Otherwise, dating is tight and consistent: #19 (9/7, pre-flood listicle) → #07 (10/7) → #20 (11/7) → #01 (12/7 flood) → #16 (13/7) → #06/#11 (14/7) → #05 (15/7) → #12 (16/7) → #14 (17/7) → #04/#13 (18/7) → #10 (19/7) → #03/#18 (20/7) → #17 (21/7) → #02 (22/7 post-flood policy) → #08 (23/7) → #15/#21/#09…(24–25/7). Every comment timestamp post-dates its article's publishedAt. The global comment relative-time anchor (0018-07-25T20:00) is after all content. ✓

## Axis C — material-culture plausibility

Within the legend frame (bronze-age Đông Sơn), the corpus is careful and largely correct.

- **Allowed & well-used:** trống đồng (#12,#16,#17), chim Lạc motif on trống/thạp (#17 + comments), thuyền độc mộc / thuyền nan / đua thuyền, nhà sàn, khố/váy, đồ đồng (rìu đồng, lưỡi cày đồng, đúc vò), barter (trâu/thóc/vải/muối), nhuộm răng / ăn trầu, cơm lam, mắm. All era-appropriate. ✓
- **Iron:** appears only as Gióng's canonical **ngựa sắt / roi sắt / áo giáp sắt** (#06) and "làng rèn … nung đồng, đúc rìu" elsewhere (the rèn villages in #16 are described forging **đồng**, not iron — nicely careful). The Phù Đổng comment even has the roi-sắt break and Gióng switch to bamboo — canon-faithful. Legend trumps archaeology here, as the brief allows. ✓
- **Units/measures:** sải tay, tầm (tay với), canh giờ / giờ Thìn–Tỵ–Mùi, tuần trăng, đấu (gạo), thúng, nong, vò, vại, bồ — all plausibly archaic and used consistently. No anachronistic standardized units leak into Văn Lang itself (the *standardized* cân/thước appear only as the *foreign* northern power's novelty in #10, which is the point). ✓
- **"Quan/officials" voice — judgment calls:**
  - Generic functionaries — **quan coi nước, quan coi chợ, tuần đinh, lạc hầu, lạc tướng, bố-chính-adjacent "quan coi việc…"** — are pragmatic newspaper-voice compromises and map onto the genuine Hùng-era structure (Vua Hùng – Lạc hầu – Lạc tướng – bộ). Acceptable. ✓
  - **"Thư lại"** (#11 ×2): a clerical/scribe title with a later (Hán-bureaucratic) flavour, and it implies record-keeping/writing. MEDIUM-LOW: it's a newspaper-voice convenience but is the single most "imported institution"-flavoured word in the corpus. Tolerable; if minimizing, "người ghi án / người chép việc" would be safer than "thư lại."
  - **"Khoa thi" / "tuyển sinh" / "hồ sơ" / "đăng ký"** (#11 title, #18 section "tuyển-sinh"): this is the deliberate deadpan-modern-grammar joke (the kén-rể reported as an admissions cycle). **Verdict: acceptable as register, NOT an era breach** — it's the same conceit as "bất-động-sản / đất nền" (#09) and "khởi nghiệp / marketing" (#07), which the project explicitly sanctions. The joke is in the *framing vocabulary*, not in any claimed Văn Lang institution (there is no actual exam, no Nho-học, no chữ-nghĩa test — just lễ vật). It does NOT import khoa cử/Confucian examination as an in-world reality. Keep. ✓ (Only #18's *section slug* "tuyen-sinh" + #11's "khoa thi" lean hardest into it; harmless.)
- **#09 "đất nền / quy hoạch / sốt đất / đầu cơ":** modern real-estate register over barter land-swaps. Same sanctioned deadpan device; the *mechanics* (đổi trâu/muối lấy đất triền cao) are period-correct. ✓
